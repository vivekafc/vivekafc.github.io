Reading this article got me thinking about how my peers and I view images nowadays. In my opinion, the thing we all have in common is this extreme exposure to memes on a daily basis. I can't think of the last time I went a day without seeing a regurgitated or new meme. Memes themselves call for this remix and regurgitation as it provides the user with a template, after which they are free to write their own captions. These memes have much more power than we think they do. For example, this past month, I have witnessed a sudden uprise of "Ok, Boomer" memes. They are tongue-in-cheek and used to reference people of a past generation who don't really have a grasp of modern technology or politics and are stuck in their times. It reached the point where boomers themselves began to retaliate. The website Know Your Meme claims that the exact origin of the phrase is currently unknown. On 4chan, the retort was first used by an anonymous /r9k/ user on September 3rd, 2015. If this is true, then this means that a comment on a thread from close to five years ago is now one of the biggest meme formats in 2019. This is all due to regurgitation of the screenshot which spread around, 4 years later.
